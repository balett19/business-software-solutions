The most popular accounting software for small businesses is QuickBooks. However, it may not be the best option for every small business. The cost may be too high, and the features are not enough. Some other bookkeeping software picks are better options for smaller businesses. This article will highlight some of the top small business accounting software options. Xero is one of the more expensive small business bookkeeping software options, but it also has many useful features for small businesses. Its Starter plan is limited by monthly allowances, and its drop-down menus are a little clunky compared to QuickBooks. Additionally, it lacks the facility to group debtors and creditors by name. It also lacks built-in payroll and requires that subscribers get a separate add-on for multicurrency transactions. Another good small business bookkeeping software is Sunrise. It offers a free plan and a paid plan with unlimited transactions for $149 per month. Its paid plans offer additional features like time tracking, comprehensive contact records, inventory management, robust reports, and full mobile access. If you are planning to invest in a rental property, you can take advantage of the Quicken Rental Property Manager software. It is designed to keep records of income and expenses for you on a continuous basis. This will help you receive deductions and track your property's performance. This software will also help you understand the performance of your rental properties and make necessary adjustments. The software allows you to enter multiple properties into one account. In order to add more than one property to Quicken, you must fill in the property name, address, and tenant labels. Next, you must connect your Quicken account and bank account.
